# April 13

* QA Preparation for Turnover*

We began preparing the system for turnover from a QA perspective. I focused on thoroughly reviewing and testing the system to ensure all features were working correctly and met the required standards. It felt like consolidating all the testing efforts into a stable and reliable system ready for deployment.

# April 14, 2026

*Verifying System Details*

I continued refining the presentation while rechecking the system. I made sure that all features, workflows, and functions were properly tested and working as expected. This required careful validation to ensure that no issues were overlooked before the turnover.

# April 15,2026

*Final Testing and Dry Run*

This day was dedicated to final testing and preparation. I performed last-minute checks, reviewed test cases, and ensured that the system was stable. I also practiced how to present the QA findings clearly, which helped boost my confidence for the turnover.

# April 16, 2026

*System Turnover – NCIP Online Job Application*

Today was a very important milestone. We officially conducted the turnover of our system, the NCIP Online Job Application, with Sir Dean.

Before the turnover, I made sure everything was working properly—from login, application flow, and admin monitoring features. During the presentation, I explained how the system works, its purpose, and how it can help make the application process more organized and efficient.

I felt a mix of nervousness and excitement while demonstrating the system, especially knowing that this was no longer just a project but something being formally handed over. It made me more aware of the importance of reliability, usability, and clear system flow.

This experience taught me how real-world systems are not just about coding, but also about communication, presentation, and ensuring that users can actually understand and use what you built.

Overall, this day felt like a big achievement. It showed me how far I’ve come—from building features step by step to successfully delivering a complete system.
