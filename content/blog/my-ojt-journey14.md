# March 9, 2026

* Title: Face Data Management

Today, I worked on organizing how face data is handled in the system. Instead of relying on a single reference, I started thinking about storing multiple face samples and structuring them properly.

I explored simple ways to manage face data in memory, like assigning IDs and grouping embeddings. This made me realize that data structure plays a big role in making the system scalable and easier to maintain.

# March 10, 2026

* Title: Improving Recognition Stability

Today, I focused on making recognition results more stable. I noticed that sometimes the system quickly switches between “Matched” and “Unknown,” which can be confusing.

To improve this, I experimented with adding thresholds and slight delays before updating the result. This made the output feel more consistent and reliable. I learned that stability is just as important as accuracy in user-facing systems.

# March 11, 2026

* Title: Enhancing Visual Feedback

Today, I improved how the system visually presents information. I added clearer labels, better positioning for face boxes, and more noticeable indicators for recognition and emotion.

These small UI improvements made the system easier to understand at a glance. It reminded me that even strong backend logic needs good visualization to be effective.

# March 12, 2026

* Title: Smoother User Experience 

Today, I focused on improving the overall user experience. I refined transitions, reduced flickering in detection updates, and made sure the system responds smoothly during continuous use.

I realized that smoothness and responsiveness are key to making the system feel professional, especially for real-time applications like this.

# March 13, 2026

* Title: Final Adjustments and Reflection

Today, I made final adjustments to both functionality and UI. I reviewed the entire system—from camera access to detection, recognition, and emotion display—to ensure everything works together properly.

Looking back, I can see how much the system has improved from a basic setup to a more complete and interactive application. This phase made me appreciate the importance of iteration and continuous improvement.
